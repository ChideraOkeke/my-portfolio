Biography

I am a citizen of Nigeria born in Onitsha Anambra state although I hail from Ukpo Dunukofia local government area Anambra state, Nigeria. I come from a close-knit family of seven. I was born to Sir Godwin Okeke and Lady Lynda Okeke on the 18th of July 2001. I am the eldest child of five children. I have two brothers and two sisters. I attended Our Lords Shepherd International School for my primary education, it is located in Enugu state Nigeria. Then Mea Mater Elizabeth High School for my secondary education which is also located in Enugu state Nigeria. We currently live in Lagos state in Nigeria. My mother is a cooperate lawyer and my father is a business man (focusing on pharmaceuticals).

I am currently attending Fisk University, located in Nashville TN. Fisk University is a small private historically black university located in north Nashville, Tennessee. It was established in the year 1866. I am a full-time sophomore at Fisk. I am a biochemistry and molecular biology major in the pre-med pathway and also a data science minor. My specialization is going to be in pediatric surgery. Pediatric surgeons operate on children whose development ranges from the newborn stage through the teenage years. I have always loved children and working with them, caring for them and loving them is my favorite thing to do. Picking a career that has to do with taking care of little children just came naturally to me. I also have a love for computer science. I discovered my love for it in the 10th grade. My teacher introduced the program development life cycle and I was hooked. My love for computer science has not wavered despite being a biochemistry major. I believe being a biochemistry major gives me a unique perspective to tackling coding problems.

I have a lot of interests and some of them include travelling, reading, fashion, makeup, baking, cooking, cycling, and swimming. I love afrobeats music and hip-pop.
